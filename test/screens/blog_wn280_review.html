      11/24/2015-11/30/2015

      [Actual Category]

      • Nicolas Chavent, co-founder of HOT, warns at OSMF-Talk mailing list that members of HOT US Inc. may get a majority at OSMF board after current board elections. This would be bad. Blake Girardot (HOT Vice President) dissents him. The further discussion reveals multiple down sides of HOT. Mails by Severin Menard (HOT board member) and Nicolas indicate that HOT voting members who disagree the HOT doctrine are rebuked. Severin writes that a procedure for violating HOT Code of Conduct has been started against Nicolas.

      • [Osmf-talk] Street Cred - required for a board position?

      • [Osmf-talk] The 'Paying People' question

      • Blake tries to stop the discussion started by Nicolas by saying it were off-topic. Severin disagrees with Blake.

      • Christoph Hormann reasons why being a OSMF board member might not be compatible with being a HOT Voting Member. (Note: Three of the candidates are HOT Voting Members)

      • Frederik Ramm: Trademark – Re: [Osmf-talk] Balancing the presence of the Humanitarian Open...

      • On occasion of the current board elections, Gregory Marler has published an analysis about the number and kind of questions and the lenght of the board terms.

      • Henk Hoff: [Osmf-talk] Past - present - future

      • OSMF Board election canidate positions

      • OSMF members who have been member for more than 30 days before the elections can give their votes for the four vacant seats at OSMF board. The single transferable vote system is used. There is a list of the candidates at OSM wiki and a questions and answer page.

      About us

      • Michael Reichert has written a review of the candidates of current OSMF board elections. (part 1, part 2)

      Mapping

      • German only

      • German only

      • Dominic Coletti asks at Tagging mailing list for comments on his proposal office=adotion_agency.

      • Clemens Schüller asks (Google Translate) at Talk-at mailing list how to map the times when the kitchen of a restaurant is manned (i.e. when warm meals are served).

      • A pull request to the iD editor by Bryan Housel makes it possible that aerial images better than Bing are now displayed as default instead of Bing. Up to now, users had to switch manually from Bing to the better local aerial image provider. A comment by Mateusz Konieczny explains why this is important. The modification will be rolled out at osm.org with the next iD release.

      • Clifford Snow asks the tagging mailing list for the best way to map sidewalks so new mappers can easily add tags for (wheelchair) routing.

      • Flanders Geographical Information Agency (FGIA) renews its services for aerial images. The service is better known under its Dutch acronym AGIV in Flanders. Only the "orthofoto's" and some parts of the historical maps can be used during mapping.

      • While you can't use aerial imagery to map turn restrictions, you can use Mapillary instead. User jinalfoflia of Mapbox Data Team wrote a guide which describes her mapping.

      • User Porjo remembers that Steve Coast announded the open access to Bing imagery for OpenStreetMap mapping five years ago.

      • OpenStreetMap Ireland congratulates Stephen who has mapped more than 10,000 townlands.

      • Vervollständigen von Daten mit Hilfe der JOSM-Suche

      Community

      • Jothirnadh of MapBox describes how he completes missing cities in India with the help of the public, free database of the country. In the comments is asked how he adds the missing information, whether it is a city or a village.

      • Did you already obtain your chrismas gifts for this year? No? Then you should have a look at malenki's summary and go buy your presents using the affiliate links from Amazon (UK and US)

      • Simon Poole analyses how large the national communities are. Germany is the largest community (103,673 mappers), followed by US (55,919), France (33,528), Russia (32,422) and Great Britan (27,750). But these numbers do not have an effect on the data quality at OSM. This is influenced by the normalised number of mappers. Austria leads (1.307 mappers per 1,000 inhabitants), followed by Germany (1.278), Luxemburg (1.045) and Switzerland (0.920).

      • Users Nighto declared in his user blog how he transmits (free) videos in Mapillary using OpenStreetMap.

      • GOwin reports about a mapping activities in the Philippines.

      • OpenCageData-Blog interviewed Matthias aka !i!

      Imports

      • User bdiscoe reports about his cleanup work of the NHD import in North Caroline. This import from the year 2009 was done badly, contained a huge number of redundancies (the reason the import was discovered in the first place) and the accuracy was bad, too.

      • Mapbox Data Team has published a detailed report about its manual cleanup efforts on the messy data which has been imported by the poor documented Yahoo Import in 2011. (We reported)

      Events

      • On 3. December, the International Day of Persons with Disabilites, the mapping event MapMyDay for more accessability takes place. The Whorld Health Organisation and Wheelmap call to map accessible places with the Wheelmap-App.

      • State of the Map 2016 will be held from September 23 to 25 in Brussels

      Humanitarian OSM

      • Students at Portmarnock Community School started a 24 hour mapping event around the #MapLesotho initiative.

      • OpenStreetMap day at Kennedy Community School in St. Joseph, Minnesota. USA

      Maps

      • The developers of OpenRailwayMap ponder on using OpenHistoricalMap as a data source for abandoned and razed railways instead of OpenStreetMap. Michael asks at Historical mailing list what to consider when copying data from OSM to OHM.

      Open Data

      • Erasmuspreis 2015 für die Community der Wikipedia

      • The Surveying Authorities of Ireland and Northern Ireland have released records as Open Data under the CC-BY 4.0. The data are still not available for OSM.

      Software

      • OSMBuildings includes a new option to print on a region of a map via 3YOURMIND directly in 3D. (via @OSMBuildings)

      • The offline OSM based app maps.me offers now spoken routing instructions. You can also define start and destination now freely on the map for a route calculation.

      Programming

      • Hypercubeplatforms published FIND."the Framework for Internal Navigation and Discovery". It is a software to locate a position with a smartphone or a laptop using WiFi signals in buildings.

      • Leaflet.Icon.Glyph

      • mapsforge 0.6.0 released

      • Vladimir Agafonkin (Mapbox) is blogging about new features and optimisations of the geoprocessing library "TileReduce"

      • Searching OpenStreetMap with Elasticsearch; a presentation from Christoph Lingg.
